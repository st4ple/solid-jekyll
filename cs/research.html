HIGHLIGHTS OF THE DEPARTMENT COMPUTER SCIENCE RESEARCH

Computer Science department is engaged and committed to incept projects of institutional and national importance in the themes of Research, Innovation and Technology Transfer. These projects involve local, regional and international collaborations and networks and building strategic partnerships.

The overarching aims of the projects are to:

  • Strengthen the Department of Computer Science and University's research capacity- this is to provide a concrete realization of the university research strategy and vision;
  • Stimulate multidisciplinary intra and inter faculty research,
  • Enhance local, regional and international collaborations and to provide outreach and visibility.
  • Develop capacity in Innovation, Technology Transfer and Intellectual Property
  • Stimulation of grassroots innovation and interfacing with Technology Transfer Offices and Incubators.

e-Health RESEARCH CLUSTER

The main aim of the research cluster is to provide a centre to bring together people and projects which are concerned with undertaking research in the field of health informatics, especially mobile health which is relevant for a country with low resources such as Botswana.
The cluster has secured a laboratory at the Faculty of health Sciences which currently has 5 Personal computers dedicated to 5 specific projects.

MOBILE COMPUTING RESEARCH AND INNOVATION LABORATORY

The department is setting up a Mobile Computing Research and Innovation Lab (MCRL).
MCRL aims to provide capacity building and upskilling of researchers, innovators, and entrepreneurs to help in exploring technology intervention in solving local problems.
The proposed lab will stimulate research and innovation in mobile computing and applications to find technology solutions of local relevance across sectors of health, education, agriculture, tourism, transport and others. The proposed lab will also aim to impart technical skills to students, entrepreneurs and innovators through capacity building and technical training activities in partnership with Industry and international strategic partners through co-creation platforms.
The lab aims to achieve this by being a center that gives mobile application developers a platform to develop mobile applications, validate and to launch them into the virtual mobile market ecosystems.

INTELLIGENT SYSTEMS RESEARCH LAB

The planned Intelligent Systems Research lab will be concerned with design and analysis of autonomous agents. In modern software design, Intelligent Systems are becoming more and more crucial to success of systems, because the types of problems encountered require dealing with very complex problems (such as automated driving), complex data, as well as large quantities of data, and real time decision making. The lab is being set up under management by the Decision and Intelligent Systems cluster in the department.
The planned Intelligent Systems Research lab focus will be on both theoretical and applied research. Theoretical research will look into issues such as support for faster search algorithms, with appropriate data structures and heuristics. Applied research will look at problem solving in areas that are of great relevance Botswana as well as Southern Africa, such as health care, mining, education, and business. The lab will be supported by an undergraduate stream in the Bachelor of Computer Science programme in the department. We expect to support undergraduate research, through final year projects, to better prepare undergraduate students for graduate research. Naturally, the MSc and PhD programmes will feed into the lab. There is already great interest among graduate students to pick topics in Artificial Intelligence, due to it being a hot research area.

HIGH PERFORMANCE COMPUTING

The Computer Science department is setting up a High Performance Computing (HPC) Data centre and infrastructure to provide a HPC-As-A-Service to the university. The department has a vision to develop capacity for teaching and research in High Performance Computing and Big Data Science and to concretely contribute to the University of Botswana research intensification strategy.

The HPC facility aims to provide a consolidated service for specialized HPC computational resource requirements across the university and to immediately provide research level computational resources to the Faculty of Science particularly in Computational Chemistry, Physics and Computational Science, Mathematical modelling.
